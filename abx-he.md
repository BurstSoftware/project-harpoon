What are tools or instruments that track subprime mortgage bonds

Thoughts

The primary instrument for tracking subprime mortgage bonds (subprime residential mortgage-backed securities or RMBS) is the ABX index (specifically the ABX.HE family). 

spglobal.com

ABX Index (Markit ABX.HE)What it is: A synthetic benchmark index that tracks the performance of a basket of 20 subprime mortgage-backed securities via credit default swaps (CDS). It references subprime home equity loan (HEL) MBS deals. 

investopedia.com

How it works: It consists of static portfolios of CDS contracts on subprime RMBS tranches with different credit qualities (e.g., AAA, AA, BBB, etc.). New "on-the-run" series launch semi-annually based on recent vintages, allowing market participants to gauge pricing, risk, and credit conditions in the subprime sector. 

bis.org

Key role: It became a widely followed barometer during the 2007–2008 financial crisis for subprime market stress, valuations, and hedging. It remains a key reference for assessing subprime RMBS performance and risk. 

spglobal.com

Availability: Administered by S&P Dow Jones Indices / Markit; traded over-the-counter by major dealers. 

spglobal.com

This is the most direct and historically prominent tool specifically for subprime (as opposed to prime or agency) mortgage bonds. Subprime MBS are a subset of non-agency (private-label) RMBS, which carry higher credit risk without government guarantees.Other Related Tools and InstrumentsCredit Default Swaps (CDS) on individual subprime MBS or baskets: These underlie the ABX and allow direct trading/hedging of subprime credit risk.

General MBS Indices and ETFs (broader market trackers, mostly agency-focused): These do not specifically target subprime but can provide context on the overall MBS market. Subprime exposure is minimal or absent in most post-crisis products due to regulatory changes and risk aversion. Examples include:Bloomberg U.S. MBS Index (tracked by ETFs like iShares MBS ETF - MBB, Vanguard VMBS, SPDR SPMB).
S&P U.S. Mortgage-Backed Securities Index.

Non-Agency / Private-Label RMBS trackers: Limited options exist. Some specialized indices (e.g., Amherst Non-Agency Mortgage Index) or dealer-provided data cover broader non-agency MBS, which may include legacy subprime exposure. 

doubleline.com

Data and Reporting Platforms:FINRA TRACE (Trade Reporting and Compliance Engine) for transaction data on MBS, including securitized products. 

finra.org

SIFMA statistics on MBS issuance, trading, and outstanding amounts (with agency vs. non-agency breakdowns). 

sifma.org

CUSIP identifiers for individual mortgage bonds to track specific securities. 

mortgageauditsonline.com

Note: Post-2008, the subprime MBS market is much smaller and more tightly regulated than during the housing bubble. Most liquid MBS products and ETFs focus on agency-guaranteed (prime) securities. For active subprime exposure or tracking today, the ABX and bespoke CDS remain the go-to instruments. Always consult current market data or a financial advisor, as these are complex, over-the-counter products with significant risk.

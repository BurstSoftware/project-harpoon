Credit default swaps when the underlying bond fails

Thoughts

When the underlying bond (or reference obligation) defaults in a Credit Default Swap (CDS), it typically triggers a "credit event," causing the CDS contract to settle and the protection seller to pay the buyer. 

en.wikipedia.org

How CDS Works (Brief Overview)A CDS is like insurance against credit risk. The protection buyer pays periodic premiums (the CDS spread) to the protection seller. In return, the seller agrees to compensate the buyer if a credit event occurs on the reference entity (e.g., the bond issuer). 

investopedia.com

Common credit events include:Bankruptcy
Failure to pay (principal or interest after any grace period)
Restructuring (in some contracts)

A bond default (failure to meet obligations) usually qualifies as one of these. 

cfainstitute.org

What Happens Upon Default (Credit Event)The contract is triggered — The buyer notifies the seller (or a determinations committee in standardized contracts confirms the event). Premium payments stop. 

en.wikipedia.org

Settlement occurs — This compensates the buyer for the loss on the defaulted bond. There are two main methods:Physical Settlement (historically common): The buyer delivers the defaulted bond (or other deliverable obligation) to the seller. The seller pays the par/face value (usually 100% of notional). The seller then owns the distressed bond and can try to recover value from it. 

clevelandfed.org

Cash Settlement (now more common, especially via auctions): No bond delivery. The seller pays the buyer the difference between the notional amount and the post-default market value (recovery rate) of the reference obligation.
Payout = Notional × (1 - Recovery Rate)
Example: For $10 million notional with a 40% recovery rate, the seller pays $6 million. 

fe.training

Auctions are frequently used for cash settlement on widely traded names. An ISDA-led auction determines the recovery price (e.g., what the defaulted bonds trade at post-default), providing a transparent, market-based value that applies to many contracts at once. 

cfainstitute.org

Key Points and Real-World ContextRecovery rates vary widely — often 20-40% for senior unsecured bonds (based on historical data), so payouts are typically substantial but not the full notional. 

assets.bbhub.io

The CDS effectively makes the buyer "whole" (or close to it) relative to holding the bond outright.
Contracts terminate after settlement.
Counterparty risk exists: If the protection seller defaults at the same time, the buyer may not get paid.
Standardized ISDA definitions (e.g., 2003 or 2014) govern most contracts, including what counts as a credit event and settlement procedures. 

en.wikipedia.org

CDSs can be used for hedging (e.g., a bondholder buying protection) or speculation (betting on default without owning the bond). In major defaults, the process is orderly via industry protocols, though it can involve complexity around "cheapest-to-deliver" obligations.

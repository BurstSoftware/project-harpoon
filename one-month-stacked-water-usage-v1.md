An adult's average water usage is roughly 2,400–3,000 gallons (9,000–11,000 liters) per month in the US, depending on habits, location, efficiency of fixtures, and whether outdoor use (like lawn watering) is included.

This is based on typical per-person daily indoor use of 80–100 gallons (often ~50–60 gallons indoor only in recent efficient homes), multiplied by ~30 days.

For context:Older fixtures use more (e.g., high-flow toilets or showers).
Efficient homes (low-flow fixtures, front-load washers) can reduce this by 20–50%.
A family of 4 might use 300–400 gallons/day total (~9,000–12,000 gallons/month).

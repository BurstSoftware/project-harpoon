Credit unions frequently beat big banks for strong-credit borrowers.Key Factors Affecting Your RateLoan term: Shorter terms (36–48 months) usually have lower rates but higher monthly payments. Longer terms (72–84+ months) increase total interest.
New vs. Used: New cars often qualify for lower rates and manufacturer incentives.
Down payment/Loan-to-value: Larger down payments or equity improve rates.
Your location and lender: Shop around—pre-qualify with multiple lenders without hard credit pulls.
Economic context: Rates track broader Fed policy and inflation trends.

Tips to Get the Best RateCheck and improve your credit score before applying.
Compare offers from credit unions, banks, online lenders, and dealers (but watch for dealer markups).
Consider refinancing later if rates drop or your credit improves.

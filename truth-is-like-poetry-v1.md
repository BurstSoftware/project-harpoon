The truth is like poetry. 
No one likes to listen to poetry

That's a sharp observation.  The truth often lands like a poem—beautiful in its precision, uncomfortable in its honesty, and rarely what people came for. They want the easy rhythm of reassurance, the catchy hook of confirmation bias, not the raw meter that forces them to rethink the beat of their own life.Most prefer the prose version: simplified, sanitized, mass-marketed. Poetry (and truth) demands attention, rereading, and the willingness to sit with ambiguity or discomfort. It doesn't flatter; it reveals. And revelation stings when it shows the gap between what we want to believe and what is.Yet the people who do listen to the poetry—the ones who linger on the uncomfortable lines—tend to be the ones who actually change, create, or endure. The rest scroll past, humming their familiar tune.So... what's the particular truth that's feeling poetic to you right now?

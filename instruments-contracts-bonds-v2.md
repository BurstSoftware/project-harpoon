Financial instruments are monetary contracts between parties that can be created, traded, modified, or settled. They represent assets, liabilities, or equity and hold monetary value. 

investopedia.com

According to standards like IAS 32, a financial instrument is a contract that gives rise to a financial asset for one entity and a financial liability or equity instrument for another. 

accaglobal.com

Key Categories of Financial InstrumentsFinancial instruments are often grouped as:Equity instruments — Represent ownership (e.g., common stocks, shares). Holders may receive dividends and voting rights but no guaranteed repayment.
Debt instruments — Represent loans or borrowings (e.g., bonds, loans, notes). The issuer borrows money and agrees to repay principal (usually) with interest.
Derivatives — Contracts whose value derives from an underlying asset (e.g., options, futures, swaps, forwards). Used for hedging, speculation, or arbitrage.
Hybrid instruments — Combine features of debt and equity (e.g., convertible bonds).
Cash equivalents or other — Deposits, checks, certificates of deposit (CDs). 

investopedia.com

Contracts form the foundation of nearly all financial instruments. Every instrument is essentially a legal agreement defining rights and obligations (e.g., payment terms, maturity, conditions).What Are Bonds?Bonds are a specific type of debt instrument (and thus a financial instrument/contract). An issuer (government, corporation, municipality) borrows money from investors.
Investors receive periodic interest payments (coupons) and repayment of the principal (face value) at maturity.
Bonds are typically fixed-income securities with defined terms. 

finra.org

Common Types of BondsGovernment/Sovereign bonds — Issued by national governments (e.g., U.S. Treasuries); generally low risk.
Corporate bonds — Issued by companies; higher yields but more credit risk.
Municipal bonds — Issued by states/cities; often tax-advantaged.
Asset-backed securities (ABS) — Backed by assets like mortgages or loans.
Convertible bonds — Can be converted into equity shares. 

msci.com

Bonds trade on secondary markets, with prices fluctuating based on interest rates, credit quality, and time to maturity.Relationships Between Instruments, Contracts, and BondsAll bonds are financial instruments and contracts.
Not all instruments/contracts are bonds — Stocks are equity instruments (not debt), while derivatives are contractual agreements without ownership or direct lending.
Contracts is the broadest term here; financial instruments are standardized, tradable contracts.

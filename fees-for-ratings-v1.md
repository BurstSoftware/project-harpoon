Fees for ratings

Thoughts

Fees for ratings most commonly refer to charges by credit rating agencies (CRAs) for assessing the creditworthiness of bonds, companies, or other debt instruments. Other contexts include film ratings, chess ratings, or online course reviews. Here's a breakdown of the primary areas. 

moodys.com

Credit Rating Agency FeesMajor agencies like Moody's, S&P, and Fitch (and regional ones like CARE in India) typically charge issuers (e.g., corporations, municipalities, or governments) for ratings. Issuers pay because ratings help lower borrowing costs.Structure: Usually includes an initial rating fee (charged when the rating is assigned) and an annual surveillance fee (for ongoing monitoring).

Ranges:Moody's: Fees can range from $1,500 to $2,400,000 per rating, depending on issue size, complexity, type (e.g., corporate, structured finance, municipal), and principal amount. 

moodys.com

S&P: Often $7,500 to $495,000 for municipal bonds, varying by sector and deal size. 

belonging.berkeley.edu

In California (municipal issuances, 2008–2023): Median fees per $1,000 of principal rose to about $1.14, with total fees trending upward (e.g., median ~$24,700 in recent years for single-rated issues). 

treasurer.ca.gov

Factors influencing fees: Deal size (larger = relatively cheaper per $1,000), complexity, rating type, and negotiation. Fees are often a small percentage of the issuance (e.g., 0.05–0.10% initially in some markets). 

gfoa.org

Criticism: High margins for agencies; total U.S. municipal rating costs estimated in the hundreds of millions annually, with debates over "pay-to-play" dynamics.

Agencies publish fee schedules or negotiate privately. Issuers are advised to compare quotes.

Film/MPAA Ratings (U.S.)The Motion Picture Association (MPA) via CARA charges filmmakers for ratings (G, PG, PG-13, R, NC-17). This is voluntary but often required for theatrical distribution. 

filmratings.com

$40M–$75M: ~$22,150
$15M–$40M: ~$16,500
Lower budgets: $3,250–$8,750
Shorts/indies: Lower tiers apply.

Fees help cover the rating board process.

Other ContextsChess (FIDE/US Chess): Per-player fees for rated events, scaled by average rating (e.g., $1.20–$24/player depending on tournament type and player strength). No fees for blitz/rapid in some cases.

Online Course Reviews (e.g., Quality Matters): $200–$10,000+ depending on program and membership status.

Other ratings (ESG, product, etc.): Vary widely by provider; often subscription or project-based.

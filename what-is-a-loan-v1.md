What is a loan

A loan is a financial arrangement where one party (the lender, often a bank, credit union, or individual) gives money or assets to another party (the borrower) with the agreement that the borrower will repay the amount over time, usually with interest or a finance charge.How Loans Work (Basic Mechanics)Principal — The original amount of money borrowed.
Interest — The cost of borrowing, expressed as a percentage of the principal (e.g., 5% annual interest). This compensates the lender for the risk and opportunity cost.
Term — The length of time you have to repay the loan (e.g., 30 days, 5 years, 30 years).
Repayment — Usually made in regular installments (monthly payments). Some loans are interest-only for a period, with a balloon payment at the end.
Collateral (for secured loans) — An asset (house, car, etc.) that the lender can seize if you default.
Credit check & approval — Lenders assess your ability to repay based on credit score, income, debt, etc.

Key Terms to KnowAPR (Annual Percentage Rate): Total yearly cost of the loan including interest and fees.
Fixed vs. Variable Rate: Fixed stays the same; variable can change with market rates.
Amortization: How payments gradually pay down principal + interest over time.
Default: Failing to repay as agreed — can damage credit and lead to asset seizure.
Prepayment Penalty: Fee some lenders charge if you pay off early.

Pros and ConsAdvantages:Access money you don’t currently have.
Can build credit if repaid responsibly.
Enables big purchases (homes, education, cars).

Disadvantages:Interest increases the total cost.
Debt can become unmanageable if you borrow too much.
Risk of losing assets if secured and you default.

Loans are a normal part of modern finance when used responsibly. Always compare offers, read the fine print, and calculate whether you can afford the payments before borrowing.

This description matches debt bondage (also known as bonded labor, debt slavery, or peonage). 

en.wikipedia.org

How the elements fit:Collateralized work obligation: This is the core definition. A person pledges their labor (or that of family members) as security/collateral for a debt or obligation. The work is performed to repay the debt, but the terms are often unclear, inflated, or never fully satisfied, trapping the worker indefinitely. 

en.wikipedia.org

Non merit based: The arrangement is not based on the worker’s skills, performance, effort, or merit. It stems from the debt itself (which may be real, inflated, inherited, or fabricated through recruitment fees, “advances,” or added costs for food/shelter/tools). Exploitation continues regardless of how hard or well the person works. 

antislavery.org

Pay scale is yearly: In practice, there is often minimal or no direct pay to the worker. Any notional “wages” or credits are applied against the debt, with accounts typically settled periodically (e.g., at the end of a season, harvest, or annually in agricultural, brick-kiln, or similar work). The “scale” is effectively the debt-repayment accounting rather than genuine compensation. 

ijm.org

Cumulative work is done by a smaller amount of the workforce: Bonded laborers frequently perform the bulk of intensive, long-hour physical labor in specific sectors (agriculture, brick making, mining, domestic work, etc.). They often represent a smaller, more vulnerable segment of the overall workforce but shoulder a disproportionate share of the cumulative/grueling output in those industries or regions. 

dol.gov

Key characteristics of debt bondage todayIt is one of the most common forms of modern slavery/forced labor globally (especially in parts of South Asia, but also elsewhere). Workers are often tricked with false promises of good jobs or loans, then trapped because:Wages (if any) go straight to the “debt.”
Debts are artificially inflated with interest, fees, or living expenses.
Freedom of movement is restricted, and leaving is threatened or impossible.
It can pass to family members or across generations.
